About Me

My name is Brian Williams, and I'm a Computer Science student with a passion for game development and gameplay programming. I've always been interested in understanding how games work, from the systems that drive gameplay to the visual effects that bring worlds to life.


I originally started learning game development in Unity. Through personal projects and experimentation, I learned the fundamentals of game development including player movement, UI systems, collision handling, game loops, scene management, and C# scripting. Those projects gave me a strong foundation and helped me understand how games are structured.


As I continued learning, I became increasingly interested in gameplay systems, visual effects, and lower-level programming. That led me to transition into Unreal Engine 5, where I could work with C++, Blueprints, Niagara, and many of the powerful tools Unreal provides for building interactive experiences.


Since moving to Unreal Engine, I've focused on creating inventory systems, interaction frameworks, UI systems, gameplay mechanics, and Niagara visual effects. Much of my work revolves around building reusable systems that can be expanded as projects grow.


Today I'm continuing to expand my knowledge of Unreal Engine, gameplay architecture, C++, visual effects, and multiplayer development while building a portfolio that showcases my growth as a developer.
